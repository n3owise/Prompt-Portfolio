## âš ï¸ Technical Challenges Faced

### **The Grid Consistency Problem**
When creating 2x2 grids with AI, making changes to ONE panel often affected OTHER panels unexpectedly, even when not mentioned in the prompt.

**Solution Approach:**
- Generate first image completely
- Download and save it
- Make incremental changes to single panels
- Repeat entire process if consistency breaks
- Sometimes had to start from scratch multiple times

### **Complexity Comparison: V60 vs Perfume Unboxing**

**V60 Coffee Grid (Simpler):**
- Objects were straightforward to generate
- Fewer intricate details to maintain
- Natural props (coffee, grinder, kettle)
- More forgiving on consistency

**Perfume Unboxing Grid (More Complex):**
- âš ï¸ **Shadow Consistency**: Maintaining identical shadows across frames
- âš ï¸ **Box Details**: Cardboard texture, fold lines, lid position
- âš ï¸ **Bottle Placement**: Exact positioning of 4 identical bottles
- âš ï¸ **Hand Continuity**: Natural progression of hand movements
- âš ï¸ **Lighting Precision**: Dramatic lighting that stays consistent
- âš ï¸ **Material Realism**: Glass bottles, cardboard box, mist spray

### **Key Learnings**
1. **Micro-details matter** in product photography prompts
2. **Explicit consistency rules** are crucial for multi-panel work
3. **Iterative approach** is necessary for commercial-quality outputs
4. **Post-processing** (Upscayl upscaling) enhances final quality
5. **Patience required** - sometimes starting over yields better results than forcing edits
